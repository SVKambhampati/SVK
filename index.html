About me

Built things. Broke things. Fixed things.
I like knowing how they actually work.

I'm a CS student at Northeastern University (AI concentration) who's always been more interested in how systems are built than in what they produce — how they scale, where they break, and what it takes to make them reliable in the real world.

I've worked across research labs, public-sector IT, and product teams — building distributed XR platforms, backend integrations, and data pipelines. Most recently I'm leading SentimentSignal at Oasis@Northeastern: a real-time NLP engine that classifies social media signals across 50+ equities and surfaces sentiment shifts before they hit price action.

Outside of engineering: badminton, movies, and way too much gaming.
